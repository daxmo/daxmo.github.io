About Me

Hi, I’m Dax. I have spent the past four years at Utah State University developing a diverse set of interests and talents. I have also spent the past two years as a communications associate for a local think tank. Between my university studies and my work experience I have gained insights from multiple perspectives and many schools of thought. My passions lie in technology and visual communication.

I believe an ideal scholarly experience should, at the very least, consist of curiosity-driven learning, mentorship, and exposure to new people and new ideas. This also directly translates to my perspectives on work. A work experience should be fostered by inspiring leaders that encourage curiosity and failure. I believe that an exposure to a diverse set of co workers with many backgrounds and experiences promotes intellectual curiosity. The best ideas often come from outside the field, from drawing on seemingly unrelated experiences.

This is why I strive to obtain an eclectic set of skills and experiences. This is also why I travel. There is no better way to find new ideas and ways of thinking than exploring the vast ranges of mountains and cultures that span the globe.

I am an admirer of many things: minimalism, photography, design, technology, architecture, sports, yoga, coffee, longboards, cats, cars, tiny homes, podcasts, motion design, music, and goats, among many others. Each of these form an important part of my work and life philosophy and drive my actions.
